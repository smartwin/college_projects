The City

The city of Montreal is the second biggest city in Canada and the largest city in the providence of Quebec. The name of the city comes from the triple-peaked hill in the heart of the city, called Mount Royal. Montreal includes the majority of the island the city resides on, as well as, few of the neighboring islands around it. Montreal is predominantly a French speaking city and as the cultural capital in Canada, it is bound to offer the everyday traveller the true Canadian experience. One of the prime things that makes Montreal so great is that the city is perfect to visit anytime throughout the year. With the underground city to beat the harsh, Candaian winters and the many unique neighborhoods for when the weather is divine, there will always be something to do in Montreal.

Underground City

Officially called Réso, the Underground City is the perfect place to visit during the winter. Located in Montreal's Central Business District, the Underground City is home to hotels, nightclubs, and the Bell Centre Hockey Rink, among other things. The largest underground system in Canada, the Réso is the must go-to place for anyone interested in shopping. It has more than 5 different shopping centers that will fulfill every traveler's shopping needs. Access to the underground city is primarily found in Montreal's metro system. Mile End

Even through this 10 block neighborhood could arguably be the hipster capital of Montreal, it is also a foodie's paradise. There are many independent restaurants along this neighborhood that will cater to every traveller's taste, ranging from vegan and meals with a modern twist to the classic french cuisine and very familiar bagels. Outside of eating, traveler's can also experience many independently owned shops and galleries throughout the ten block neighborhood. A top ranking neighborhood among travel sites, Mile End is the perfect place to take the family and experience Montreal to its fullest. Click here for more information.

Le Village

Known as the Gay Village, Le Village is the perfect place to enjoy the nightlife of Montreal, whether gay or not. Located just east of downtown, travelers can get the full experience of Le Village during the summer months of June through September. During the summer vehicles are not allowed along the main street of the village (Sainte-Catherine Street); therefore, travellers can fully experience the food and restaurants that the heart of the village has to offer. In addition, Le Village is known for its unique shops and boutiques that are experiences in and of themselves. as well as witness the many kinds of art that is displayed during the summer months. The party is at its peak in August for Montreal's Pride Week. Click here for more information.

Montreal Botanical Gardens

Located near the Biodôme and Olympic Park, the Montreal Botanical Garden ignites the green thumb in every traveler. In the fall, many of the 22,000 plant species are in bloom, making it the best time to go; however, it is smart to buy tickets in advance to avoid the long lines. During the day, families can enjoy The Great Pumpkin Ball and Spiders Unmasked, while at night, the Garden is lit up showcasing the many beautiful sculptures in the Chinese and Japanese Gardens. With most than 20 different gardens, the Botanical Gardens will be fun for the whole family and definitely a sight to see and experience. Click here for more information.
